What I need to know about

These are simply the things I need to know before I start work on a project, but you don’t need to include all of these details in your initial email. I’ll walk you through the process step by step and ask when I need to know something.

  • The type of text (e.g., CV, blog post, newsletter, website content).
  • The total word count (excluding the main title but including all headings and subheadings). Don’t worry if you can’t work it out—just let me know if this is the case!
  • Your personal deadline, if applicable. (This can be taken into account when we mutually agree upon the timeframe for turnaround at the outset of a project. I may offer express delivery for an extra fee.)
  • The language: British English, American English, or other. (American English only accepts -ize spellings (e.g., analyze, recognize), whereas British English accepts both -ize and -ise spellings (e.g., analyse, recognise). Therefore, if you specify British English, please also specify if you want -ize or -ise spellings—just pick whichever one you prefer or need and I will make sure your text is consistent with its use!)
  • The target audience and/or the purpose of the text.
  • Your main concern(s) (e.g., native-like fluency, SPaG, structure).
  • Your service expectations, preferences, and needs (e.g., proofread, copyedit).

Track Changes

I usually use the 'Track Changes' feature on Microsoft Word to edit your document. This way, you can see all my individual changes. I will also add comments to provide more context on certain decisions I have made, make insightful suggestions as to how you might improve upon your work, and ask any questions that I may have about the content. I often, but not always, provide a separate overflow document I call a 'feedback report' that contains more information, suggestions, and queries so that your document is not flooded with an excess of words!

I will always provide two documents:
1) the original piece of work with all my corrections suggested using Track Changes, allowing you to implement them as you please; and
2) the edited piece of work with all my corrections implemented, so you can see the final result of my work easily.

I also provide PDF versions of both these documents.

Revisions

With every service I offer at least one (but usually two or more) revision(s). That is, you can send the text(s) back to me and I can carry out another round of copyediting or proofreading. This is to ensure your utmost satisfaction with my service and confidence in your work. Often, clients will have a round of copyediting and then a round of proofreading later on once they have implemented the changes they would like to make.
